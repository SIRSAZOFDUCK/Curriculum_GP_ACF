The medical pathway is the blue band; the ACP pathway is the pink band. Use the dropdown to switch clinical domain, and click any cell for the source text and why it was scored.

This compares intended curricula on paper — what each programme is designed to deliver. It does not measure any individual practitioner's competence, exposure, or safety, and is not a claim about any person. Every cell was coded by a single automated rater (Claude) with no inter-rater check; shared openly so it can be challenged and corrected.

Medical pathway

Medical degree→ Foundation→ GP training

Endpoint: unrestricted generalist (CCT). The whole undergraduate clinical syllabus plus two Foundation years sit before GP training begins.

ACP (primary-care nurse) pathway

Nursing degree→ experience*→ ACP master's

Clinical breadth is modular: each system is included or excluded by employer-negotiated scope of practice — not a fixed whole-population syllabus.

*Post-qualification experience. Entry to ACP training typically expects a period of post-registration clinical experience — often cited as at least 36 months — between the nursing degree and the ACP master's. Because this experience is not assessed against any defined curriculum or framework and varies widely by individual and employer, it cannot be mapped at condition level and is shown only as a placeholder, not scored.
Why Foundation has no column. The UK Foundation Programme curriculum is capability-specified, not condition-specified — in its own words it establishes “generic and transferable capabilities rather than specific skills,” with no enumerated condition list. It adds the supervised-practice / entrustment layer between medical school and GP training, but contributes nothing to map at the per-condition level.

How to read this

Cells are coloured by how explicitly each curriculum specifies the condition, and carry a small badge for the depth / level the source defines. The dot meanings are in the key above. A hatched top-right corner marks an “implied–uncertain” match — wording broad enough to arguably cover the condition, logged generously to the ACP side but flagged as not confirmed. Every cell is clickable for its underlying source text and rationale.

Why the two ACP-pathway columns often look thin. The ACP primary-care framework compresses its entire clinical syllabus into one Appendix 1, which lists presenting complaints (chest pain, palpitations, oedema, blood-pressure issues) plus emergencies — it names almost no individual conditions. A broad presentation can imply several named conditions but the framework never states them. NMC pre-registration standards are a professional/holistic curriculum, not a diagnostic-condition list. Six RCGP domains have no dedicated ACP-system counterpart at all — the status strip names them when you select one.

Why “GMC Outcomes” is mostly implied, not full. It does not enumerate named conditions the way RCGP does — it states breadth as a principle (“common and important diseases”) to be applied. The MLA content map carries the medical pathway's actual named A–Z list (the floor for readiness to start work, below the GP-CCT comparator).

Limitations

Read this first. An item appearing in a curriculum does not verify it was actually taught, learned, or summatively assessed — only that the document lists it. This compares intended curricula on paper, not what any individual was taught or can do. It is not a claim about any practitioner's competence or safety.

1What this cannot tell you

  • Curriculum ≠ teaching ≠ assessment. Listing in a document doesn't confirm the content was delivered or formally examined; conversely, an “absent” item may be something a clinician handles well in practice.
  • Intended ≠ attained ≠ exposure. This is about design on paper, not absorbed competence or real-world caseload.
  • Depth is largely unstated. Even where both pathways “cover” something, the documents rarely fix the standard. The depth dots are a best-effort proxy, not a measured quantity.
  • Conditions only. This maps diagnostic conditions — not the wider skill set a GP-type role needs: consultation and communication, complexity and uncertainty, continuity, safeguarding, prescribing safety, teamwork, ethics, leadership and the professional capabilities. A thin condition column is not the whole job.

2How the method shapes the result

  • Asymmetric source granularity. RCGP lists named conditions; the ACP framework lists presenting complaints. The columns aren't strictly like-for-like, which predisposes ACP columns to look thinner. Coding is deliberately generous (breadth logged as “implied”) to compensate.
  • The yardstick is a choice. Everything is mapped onto the RCGP, privileging a medical framing of what a clinician should know. A nursing-led framing might foreground holistic/person-centred outcomes where the nursing base may exceed the medical baseline. This maps medicine→ACP only; the reverse pass is still to come.
  • Comparator inflates the apparent gap. The bar is the fully-trained GP at CCT (the completed endpoint).
  • Scope of practice is invisible in a static grid. ACP clinical breadth is modular and employer-negotiated, condition by condition. The grid shows the framework's ceiling, not any individual's agreed scope.
  • “Absent” is ambiguous. A blank can mean genuinely not in the curriculum, or present but too generic to map.

3Known gaps in this version

  • Single automated rater. Every cell was coded by Claude (an AI language model), not a human subject expert, and with no inter-rater check. The four-point code and uncertain flag make the soft judgements visible but don't remove the subjectivity — and an automated rater can misread a source or miss context a clinician would catch. Treat the coding as a first draft for human review, not an authority.
  • Frameworks, not delivered curricula. This uses the capability frameworks the ACP MSc is accredited against — not actual university module specifications, which may teach more or less.
  • One base profession, one specialty. The nurse→primary-care strand is the exemplar; other ACP backgrounds (paramedic, pharmacist, physiotherapy) and other specialties differ.
  • Currency / superseded-document risk. Sources differ in vintage (ACP 2020, RCGP 2025, NMC 2018), and the Centre for Advancing Practice credential — the current direction of travel — is not yet included.
  • Source fidelity. Several cells rest on summaries and extractions rather than line-by-line reads, and the source text in each panel is indicative/paraphrased, not verbatim. Coding errors are possible — the work is shared openly so they can be corrected.
